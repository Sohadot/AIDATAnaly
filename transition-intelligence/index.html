Transition Intelligence

Definition

Transition Intelligence is the decision-ready knowledge that transition measurement produces. It is not raw data and not a dashboard. It is the structured answer to four questions: where movement is weak, why it is weak, how confident that reading is, and where correction belongs.

A complete unit of Transition Intelligence has four parts:

  • a scored vector with a diagnostic class — for example Transition Constrained on T3,
  • a named failure mode from the governed ontology — for example tfo.t3.process_friction,
  • an evidence level reported separately from the score — for example E2 Observable Evidence,
  • and a recommended intervention layer — for example UX Intervention.

Why the Category Exists

Marketing teams rarely lack data; they lack verdicts. Analytics platforms produce measurements without interpretation, and consultants produce interpretation without governed measurement. The space between them — interpretation that is disciplined by a standard, restricted to a closed failure taxonomy, and honest about its own evidence — had no name. Transition Intelligence names that layer.

What the Category Measures

Transition Intelligence is downstream of measurement: it is produced from, and bounded by, the AIDA Transition Index. Whatever ATI cannot score, Transition Intelligence cannot claim. This is enforced by two rules it inherits directly:

  • a vector with absent evidence is reported as Unscorable — intelligence is never manufactured from missing measurement,
  • conclusions are stated at the strength of their Evidence Confidence level — provisional at E1, indicative at E2, stronger at E3 and E4, and never as guaranteed causality.

What the Category Does Not Claim

  • Transition Intelligence is not prediction. It diagnoses present movement; it does not forecast revenue.
  • It is not automation of judgment. It narrows decisions to a named failure and a correction class; humans decide the tactic.
  • It is not presented as an adopted industry practice; it is a governed layer introduced by AIDAtanaly.

Relationship to AIDA

Transition Intelligence speaks AIDA's vocabulary because the underlying measurement does: every intelligence unit is anchored to one of the four transitions between Attention, Interest, Desire, Action, and Loyalty. How AIDA is used as a measurement structure — rather than a rigid linear story — is defined at Measurement Grammar.

Relationship to ATI

The AIDA Transition Index is the measurement engine beneath this layer. ATI produces the scores, classes, and confidence levels; Transition Intelligence is the governed interpretation assembled on top of them.

Relationship to TFO

The "why" inside every intelligence unit comes from the Transition Failure Ontology. The ontology is closed: a diagnosis may only name one of the 21 governed failure modes or the Measurement Gap constraint. If a weakness does not fit the ontology, the ontology is amended through governance — the diagnosis never invents a new label.

Example Use Cases

  • A quarterly review replaces "conversion is down" with: T3 Constrained, likely Process Friction and Trust Deficit, E2 evidence, UX and Proof interventions recommended.
  • A budget debate is reframed: reach metrics are strong but T1 intelligence shows Vanity Reach — more spend would scale a weak transition.
  • A leadership team learns its T4 reading is Unscorable, and the first corrective action becomes measurement repair, not retention tactics.

Scanner Relationship

The Transition Scanner is the public generator of Transition Intelligence: each scan produces a vector profile, failure mode candidates, evidence levels, and intervention layers, with every element linking back to its canonical reference page. The production rules are public at methodology.

Related Category Terms

AIDA Transition Analytics is the discipline this intelligence belongs to. Measurement Grammar defines the structural reading of AIDA that makes the discipline possible.

Governance Notes

Transition Intelligence language is bound by the same claim discipline as every governed surface of this asset: no unsupported certainty, no revenue promises, no diagnosis outside the ontology. See governance.
