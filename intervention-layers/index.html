Intervention Layers

When the AIDA Transition Index finds weak movement and the Transition Failure Ontology names the failure, the system answers one final question: where should correction be applied? The answer is always one or more of ten approved intervention layers.

Definition

An intervention layer is a governed class of corrective action. It names the part of the funnel system where a correction belongs — the audience, the message, the offer, the proof, the path — without prescribing a specific tactic. Every failure mode in the ontology maps to its relevant layers, and every scanner recommendation is expressed in these terms.

Why This Standard Exists

Unbounded recommendations degrade trust. If a diagnostic tool may suggest anything, its suggestions drift toward generic marketing advice. A closed registry keeps every recommendation traceable: from a scored vector, through a named failure mode, to a defined layer of correction. It also keeps recommendations honest at low evidence levels — a class-level recommendation does not pretend to more precision than the evidence supports.

The Ten Approved Layers

Version 1.0 of the registry contains exactly ten layers. No custom layer may be introduced without an ontology update.

Contextual Subcases, Not Standalone Layers

Two commonly proposed layers are deliberately not standalone in v1.0:

  • Channel Intervention is governed under Audience Intervention or Message Intervention, depending on context. Changing a channel is either a change in who is reached or a change in how the message is delivered.
  • Measurement Governance is governed under Attribution Intervention, and may relate to Lifecycle Intervention when retention visibility is affected. It is the standard correction context for Measurement Gap (tfo.constraint.measurement_gap) and Loyalty Blindness.

Treating these as independent layers would fragment the registry and create overlapping recommendations. The mapping keeps them addressable without expanding the layer count.

How the Scanner Uses Layers

Scanner v1 recommends layers, not tactics. A typical output reads: "T3 appears Transition Constrained. The strongest signal points to Process Friction. Recommended intervention layers: UX Intervention, Timing Intervention." Interventions remain class-level recommendations unless sufficient evidence supports specific tactical advice — a rule inherited directly from the ATI Standard and explained on the methodology page.

What This Standard Does Not Claim

  • A layer recommendation identifies where correction belongs; it does not promise that any specific change will produce a specific result.
  • Layers are not a maturity model and not a ranking; no layer is inherently more important than another.
  • The registry does not claim to cover disciplines outside funnel movement, such as product strategy or finance.
