Value Ambiguity blocks movement on T2 Intent Formation.

Definition

Value Ambiguity occurs when prospects understand the offer at a surface level but cannot clearly translate it into a reason to choose, pay, act, or prioritize. Interest exists, but value is not decisive.

Core Diagnostic Question

Does the prospect understand why this offer is worth choosing now?

Why It Matters

When Interest → Desire fails, engagement does not become commercial desire. Value Ambiguity names a specific pattern of that failure so correction can target the right intervention layer.

Symptoms

  • Users understand the category but not the unique value
  • High engagement with explanation pages but weak intent signals
  • Prospects ask why this or why now
  • Offer appears useful but not urgent
  • Benefits are described but not made commercially compelling

Detection Signals

  • Low movement from educational pages to conversion pages
  • Weak engagement with offer or pricing sections
  • Repeated visits without intent
  • High comparison behavior
  • Low response to value proposition CTAs

Scoring Impact

Value Ambiguity primarily weakens:

  • D1 - Signal Quality
  • D2 - Movement Probability
  • D6 - Commercial Consequence
  • D7 - Intervention Clarity

Evidence Confidence Considerations

Diagnosis of Value Ambiguity requires the detection signals listed on this page to be observable, not assumed. When evidence is E1 Self-Reported, scanner output must describe the finding as provisional. When evidence is E0 Absent, the parent vector may be Unscorable rather than scored.

Stronger evidence levels permit stronger language but never imply guaranteed causality or revenue outcomes. See Evidence Confidence.
