Preference Dilution

Stable ID
tfo.t2.preference_dilution
Primary Vector
T2 - Interest to Desire (Intent Formation)
Transition Name
Interest → Desire
Canonical Route
/failure-modes/preference-dilution/
Diagnostic Class
Intent Formation Failure

Definition

Preference Dilution occurs when multiple alternatives weaken the prospect's ability to form a clear preference for the offer. Interest exists, but the offer does not become the preferred choice.

Core Diagnostic Question

Is the prospect interested but not sufficiently biased toward this offer?

Why It Matters

When Interest → Desire fails, engagement does not become commercial desire. Preference Dilution names a specific pattern of that failure so correction can target the right intervention layer.

Symptoms

  • Users compare but do not favor the brand
  • Weak differentiation
  • Generic positioning
  • High engagement with alternatives
  • No clear reason to choose
  • Sales or support questions show uncertainty about advantage

Detection Signals

  • Competitor comparison searches
  • Low conversion from comparison pages
  • Weak interaction with differentiator sections
  • High engagement with proof content but weak next-step action
  • Repeated evaluation without preference signal

Scoring Impact

Preference Dilution primarily weakens:

  • D1 - Signal Quality
  • D2 - Movement Probability
  • D6 - Commercial Consequence

Evidence Confidence Considerations

Diagnosis of Preference Dilution requires the detection signals listed on this page to be observable, not assumed. When evidence is E1 Self-Reported, scanner output must describe the finding as provisional. When evidence is E0 Absent, the parent vector may be Unscorable rather than scored.

Stronger evidence levels permit stronger language but never imply guaranteed causality or revenue outcomes. See Evidence Confidence.
