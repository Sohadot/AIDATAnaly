Sources and Reference Posture

AIDAtanaly introduces governed internal standards for transition measurement. This page explains what is internal, what is external, and what requires evidence before stronger public claims.

Source Policy

AIDAtanaly may introduce governed internal standards before market adoption, but it must not describe them as adopted industry standards unless adoption evidence exists.

The system is designed as a reference architecture first. External benchmark authority or market-wide adoption must be demonstrated - not assumed in copy.

Internal Governed Standards

These are ratified inside the AIDAtanaly governance stack:

  • AIDA Transition Index (ATI) - transition scoring standard
  • Transition Failure Ontology (TFO) - failure taxonomy
  • Evidence Confidence - qualification layer (E0-E4)
  • Intervention Layers - approved intervention classes
  • Scanner v1 - rules-governed diagnostic engine

Machine-readable mirrors live in /data/*.json and are validated against ontology and route documents at build time.

External References

External frameworks, benchmarks, or third-party methodologies may inform design thinking but do not automatically govern ATI scores, TFO IDs, or scanner output. When external sources are cited on public pages, they must be identifiable and proportionate - not used to imply adoption AIDAtanaly has not documented.

Claims Requiring Adoption Evidence

  • Claims of standard or widely adopted status.
  • Market benchmark comparisons presented as authoritative.
  • Claims that enterprises or regulators have formally adopted ATI or TFO.

Without documented adoption evidence, use language such as governed internal standard or introduced by AIDAtanaly - not industry standard.

Claims Requiring Source Support

  • Quantified performance outcomes attributed to using the scanner or reports.
  • Comparative superiority over named tools or methodologies.
  • Legal, financial, or compliance assertions beyond diagnostic scope.

Diagnostic dossiers describe movement failure patterns; they do not prove revenue causality without supported evidence and appropriate scope.

Claim Limits

Public pages and scanner output must avoid revenue guarantee language, AI omniscience claims, and fear-based urgency. Monetized reports must extend the same discipline. See Governance and Methodology.

Update Policy

When source posture or standards change, governed documents are versioned sequentially and public pages are updated to match ratified versions before indexed release.
