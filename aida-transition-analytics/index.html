AIDA Transition Analytics

Definition

AIDA Transition Analytics is the discipline of measuring the movement between funnel states — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action, and Loyalty — rather than counting the activity inside them. Its unit of measurement is the transition: the four governed vectors that connect five states.

Text alternative: AIDA Transition Analytics measures four transitions: T1 Attention to Interest, T2 Interest to Desire, T3 Desire to Action, and T4 Action to Loyalty.

Why the Category Exists

Funnel analytics, as commonly practiced, is state accounting. It reports how many people occupied each stage: impressions, visits, leads, orders. Those counts are necessary but structurally incomplete — a funnel can show healthy stage volume at every level while the movement between levels quietly degrades. The questions that determine commercial outcome live between the stages: did visibility become curiosity, did curiosity become preference, did preference become action, did action become continuity? No widely used measurement vocabulary existed for those four questions as first-class measured objects. AIDA Transition Analytics names that gap and governs the language for closing it.

What the Category Measures

  • Transition health — the quality of movement on each of the four vectors, scored against seven dimensions: signal quality, movement probability, velocity, friction resilience, drop-off resilience, commercial consequence, and intervention clarity.
  • Transition failure — when movement weakens, the specific, named way it weakens, classified by ontology rather than guessed.
  • Evidence strength — how much measurement actually stands behind each reading, reported separately from the reading itself.

What the Category Does Not Claim

  • It does not claim that AIDA is the only valid funnel model; it uses AIDA as a measurement grammar, not as a rigid linear path.
  • It does not claim to predict revenue or to certify business outcomes.
  • It does not claim adoption that has not occurred. AIDA Transition Analytics is a governed category introduced by AIDAtanaly; it is not presented as an adopted industry standard.
  • It does not replace analytics platforms, CRMs, or attribution tools; it interprets the movement evidence they produce.

Relationship to AIDA

AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action — is one of the oldest models in marketing. AIDAtanaly keeps the model's vocabulary and changes its role: the stages become measurement positions, and the analytical weight moves to the four connections between them. The model is also extended by one state, Loyalty, because action without continuity is incomplete movement intelligence. The reasoning is developed on the measurement grammar page.

Relationship to ATI

The category's measurement standard is the AIDA Transition Index (ATI). ATI defines the four vectors (T1–T4), the seven scoring dimensions, the five diagnostic classes, and the Evidence Confidence scale that qualifies every output. Where this page defines what the category is, ATI defines how it measures.

Relationship to TFO

When measurement finds weakness, the Transition Failure Ontology (TFO) classifies it: 21 governed failure modes across the four vectors, plus one cross-vector diagnostic constraint for unmeasurable movement. Each failure mode carries a stable ID, such as tfo.t3.process_friction, and maps to approved intervention layers.

Example Use Cases

  • An ecommerce store with strong traffic and weak sales uses transition measurement to locate the break: T1 is functional, T3 is constrained — the problem is not reach, it is the path from desire to checkout.
  • A SaaS team with healthy signups and quiet churn discovers that every report measures T3 and nothing measures T4 — a measurement gap, not yet proof of a retention failure.
  • An agency replaces "the funnel feels weak" with a vector profile: which transition, which failure mode, which intervention layer, at what evidence level.

Scanner Relationship

The category's public instrument is the Transition Scanner — a rules-governed diagnostic that produces a T1–T4 profile, names likely failure modes from the ontology, and reports Evidence Confidence per vector. Its method is documented on the methodology page.

Related Category Terms

Two adjacent terms complete the category language: Transition Intelligence, the decision-ready output layer that transition measurement produces, and Measurement Grammar, the reading of AIDA as a grammar for measurement rather than a rigid path.
