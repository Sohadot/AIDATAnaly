Core Diagnostic Question

Did visibility become curiosity?

Definition

T1 measures whether raw visibility becomes meaningful engagement. It governs the transition from being noticed to becoming interesting. The unit of measurement is not impressions or reach; it is whether exposure produces qualified curiosity that continues beyond first contact.

Why This Transition Matters

Every downstream transition depends on T1. If visibility does not convert to interest, T2 has nothing to crystallize into preference, T3 has no intent to convert, and T4 has no customer to retain. Teams that scale spend on weak T1 movement amplify volume without building a funnel — the classic pattern of strong reach with weak commercial movement.

Strong Signals

  • Qualified click-through
  • Meaningful scroll depth
  • Repeat visit after exposure
  • Search follow-up
  • Page continuation
  • Content interaction
  • Audience-fit engagement
  • Low bounce from qualified sources
  • Ad-to-content alignment

Weak Signals

  • High impressions with low engagement
  • Low-quality traffic
  • Accidental clicks
  • Irrelevant audience
  • Short sessions
  • High bounce
  • Social visibility without site interest
  • Curiosity gap clicks without continuation

ATI Scoring Dimensions

Every T1 score is computed from seven weighted dimensions defined in the AIDA Transition Index:

DimensionWeightT1 relevance
D1 Signal Quality17%Strength of engagement signal vs. noise
D2 Movement Probability23%Likelihood curiosity forms after exposure
D3 Transition Velocity11%Speed of interest after first contact
D4 Friction Resilience17%Clarity of first landing experience
D5 Drop-Off Resilience12%Resistance to immediate bounce
D6 Commercial Consequence10%Downstream commercial movement from T1 traffic
D7 Intervention Clarity10%Ability to identify weak channel, audience, or message

Evidence Confidence Considerations

T1 scores require observable engagement data: click quality, scroll depth, return visits, or source-linked behavior. When only reach or impression volume is available, Evidence Confidence falls to E0 or E1 and the vector may be reported as Unscorable or provisional. Reach without engagement evidence is not sufficient for a strong T1 reading.

Failure Modes Under This Vector

When T1 movement weakens, the Transition Failure Ontology classifies it into one of five governed failure modes:

Attention Noise

tfo.t1.attention_noise

Visibility does not produce meaningful engagement.

Vanity Reach

tfo.t1.vanity_reach

Large audience volume without transition quality.

Audience Mismatch

tfo.t1.audience_mismatch

Wrong audience for the offer or buyer profile.

Signal Decay

tfo.t1.signal_decay

Initial attention weakens before curiosity forms.

Message Blur

tfo.t1.message_blur

Exposure without interpretive clarity about why the offer matters.

Scanner Relationship

The Transition Scanner evaluates T1 through eight governed questions mapped to D1–D7, assigns Evidence Confidence for this vector, and names likely failure modes from the five T1 entries above only.
