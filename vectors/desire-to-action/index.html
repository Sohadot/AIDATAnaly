Core Diagnostic Question

Did intent become action?

Definition

T3 measures whether formed intent becomes completed action. It governs the transition from wanting to doing — checkout, signup, booking, purchase, demo request, or any governed conversion event defined for the business model.

Why This Transition Matters

T3 is where commercial outcome is won or lost. Desire without action is the most expensive form of funnel waste: the prospect wanted the offer, showed intent signals, and still did not complete. Teams often misdiagnose T3 weakness as a T1 traffic problem or a T2 content problem when the break is friction, trust, price exposure, or unresolved objections at the action threshold.

Strong Signals

  • Checkout completion
  • Signup completion
  • Demo request
  • Quote request
  • Contact form completion
  • Purchase
  • Subscription
  • Booking
  • Sales-qualified movement
  • Clear next-step behavior

Weak Signals

  • Cart abandonment
  • Form abandonment
  • Repeated pricing visits with no action
  • Stalled demo intent
  • Hesitation at payment
  • High exit on checkout or contact page
  • Repeated visits without commitment
  • Intent signals without conversion

Every T3 score is computed from seven weighted dimensions defined in the AIDA Transition Index:

D2 Movement Probability23%Intent users completing the next step
D3 Transition Velocity11%Action without excessive delay after intent
D4 Friction Resilience17%Simple, credible, low-friction action path
D5 Drop-Off Resilience12%Resistance to form, cart, or checkout abandonment

D7 Intervention Clarity10%Identifiable block: trust, price, UX, complexity, objections

Evidence Confidence Considerations

T3 requires conversion tracking: completed checkouts, signups, bookings, or CRM-stage progression to closed action. Without conversion events, Evidence Confidence cannot exceed E1. Observable abandonment patterns on action pages support E2; connected analytics plus CRM data supports E3.

Failure Modes Under This Vector

When T3 movement weakens, the Transition Failure Ontology classifies it into one of six governed failure modes:

Process Friction

tfo.t3.process_friction

Action path too long, confusing, slow, or broken.

Trust Deficit

tfo.t3.trust_deficit

Desire exists but trust is insufficient to act.

Price Shock

tfo.t3.price_shock

Price exposure collapses movement from desire to action.

Decision Delay

tfo.t3.decision_delay

Intent present but urgency insufficient for timely action.

Complexity Wall

tfo.t3.complexity_wall

Complexity prevents crossing the action threshold.

Objection Residue

tfo.t3.objection_residue

Unresolved doubts block action after desire forms.
