Core Diagnostic Question

Did conversion become continuity?

Definition

T4 measures whether completed action becomes continuity, repeat value, loyalty, advocacy, or durable customer relationship. It governs the transition from transaction to continuity — from a first purchase or signup to ongoing value.

AIDAtanaly extends AIDA beyond Action because action without continuity is incomplete movement intelligence.

Why This Transition Matters

A funnel that ends at Action measures acquisition, not value. One-time transactions, inactive accounts after signup, and silent churn all indicate that T3 succeeded while T4 failed. Customer lifetime value, referral, and advocacy all live on this transition. Teams that declare victory at conversion while ignoring continuity are measuring half the funnel.

Strong Signals

  • Repeat purchase
  • Product activation
  • Returning customer behavior
  • Subscription continuation
  • Referral
  • Review
  • Account expansion
  • Usage depth
  • Lifecycle email engagement
  • Low churn risk
  • Advocacy behavior

Weak Signals

  • One-time purchase
  • Inactive account after signup
  • Silent churn
  • No onboarding completion
  • No repeat engagement
  • No referral or review behavior
  • Weak lifecycle communication
  • Support contact without recovery
  • Post-purchase abandonment

ATI Scoring Dimensions

Every T4 score is computed from seven weighted dimensions defined in the AIDA Transition Index:

DimensionWeightT4 relevance
D1 Signal Quality17%Post-action behavior shows continuity, not only completion
D2 Movement Probability23%Customers return, repeat, activate, or expand
D3 Transition Velocity11%Relationship continues soon after first action
D4 Friction Resilience17%Onboarding and post-action experience are clear
D5 Drop-Off Resilience12%Customers avoid disappearing after first action
D6 Commercial Consequence10%Loyalty generates repeat value, LTV, referral, or advocacy
D7 Intervention Clarity10%Identifiable block to repeat value or loyalty

Evidence Confidence Considerations

T4 is the most frequently unmeasured vector. Without retention, churn, repeat purchase, or usage data, Evidence Confidence is often E0 and the vector is reported as Unscorable — not as a false zero. Connected acquisition-to-retention tracking supports E3; validated cohort patterns over time support E4.

Failure Modes Under This Vector

When T4 movement weakens, the Transition Failure Ontology classifies it into one of five governed failure modes:

One-Transaction Funnel

tfo.t4.one_transaction_funnel

First action happens; continuity does not.

Silent Churn

tfo.t4.silent_churn

Customers leave without visible alarm.

Loyalty Blindness

tfo.t4.loyalty_blindness

First conversion measured; loyalty is not.

Advocacy Vacuum

tfo.t4.advocacy_vacuum

Loyalty does not become market-facing proof.

Lifecycle Disconnect

tfo.t4.lifecycle_disconnect

Post-action relationship lacks structure.
