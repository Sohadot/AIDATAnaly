Category definition — how AIDA is read in this system

Measurement Grammar

Definition

A measurement grammar is a fixed set of named positions that makes movement describable and therefore measurable. AIDAtanaly reads AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action, extended by Loyalty — as exactly that: not a claim about how buyers behave, but a coordinate system for stating where movement happened, where it stalled, and where it broke.

Grammar does not dictate what people say; it makes statements interpretable. In the same way, the five states do not dictate how prospects move; they make movement measurable.

Why the Category Exists

AIDA is regularly dismissed with the same objection: real buyers do not move in a straight line. The objection is correct about behavior and wrong about measurement. Non-linear journeys still pass through describable states — a person who loops, skips, or returns is still, at any observed moment, somewhere between being unaware and being loyal. Discarding the vocabulary because journeys are messy leaves measurement with no positions at all. This page exists to state the governed resolution: keep the states as grammar, move the measurement to the transitions.

What the Category Measures

Under this grammar, the measured objects are the four transitions, not the five states:

  1. Attention
  2. T1
  3. Interest
  4. T2
  5. Desire
  6. T3
  7. Action
  8. T4
  9. Loyalty

Text alternative: five grammar states — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action, Loyalty — are connected by four measured transitions T1 through T4.

The states are deliberately quiet; they are reference positions. Everything scored, classified, and diagnosed in this system happens on the connections between them. That is the doctrine made operational:

Stages are states. Value lives in transitions.

What the Category Does Not Claim

  • It does not claim journeys are linear. Loops, skips, and re-entries are expected; the grammar locates them rather than denying them.
  • It does not claim the five states are psychological truth. They are measurement positions chosen for stability and shared vocabulary.
  • It does not claim other models are invalid. It claims only that a fixed grammar is a precondition for governed transition measurement.

Relationship to AIDA

The grammar keeps AIDA's four classical states and extends the sequence with Loyalty, because a grammar that ends at Action cannot describe the movement that determines durable value: from converting once to staying, repeating, and advocating. Action without continuity is incomplete movement intelligence — which is why the fourth measured transition, T4, exists.

Relationship to ATI

The AIDA Transition Index is the scoring system built on this grammar. The grammar contributes the positions; ATI contributes the measurement: four vectors, seven weighted dimensions, five diagnostic classes, and a separate Evidence Confidence qualifier.

Relationship to TFO

A fixed grammar is also what makes failure classifiable. Because every weakness is located on a named transition, the Transition Failure Ontology can assign it a governed identity — for example tfo.t1.attention_noise lives on T1, and tfo.t4.silent_churn lives on T4. Without stable positions, failure naming collapses into ad-hoc opinion.

Example Use Cases

  • A team debating "awareness versus conversion" budget reframes the question in grammar terms: which transition is weakest, T1 or T3 — and measures instead of arguing.
  • A non-linear B2B journey with long loops between Interest and Desire is described precisely as repeated T2 stalls, rather than dismissed as "the model doesn't fit us."
  • A post-purchase team adopts T4 vocabulary — activation, continuity, advocacy — and stops reporting retention as an afterthought of acquisition metrics.

Scanner Relationship

The Transition Scanner is organized by this grammar: its questions are grouped by transition, and its outputs locate every finding on the axis. A scan result is readable precisely because the grammar fixes what T1 through T4 mean — the same definitions documented at methodology.

Related Category Terms

AIDA Transition Analytics is the discipline built on this grammar. Transition Intelligence is the decision-ready output the discipline produces.

Governance Notes

The state names, transition names, and vector IDs are fixed by the governed standard; changing them is a major change requiring a decision log entry. See governance for the change control rules.
