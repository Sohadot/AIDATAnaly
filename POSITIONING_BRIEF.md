## 4. Problem Statement

Funnel reporting counts **occupancy**: impressions, sessions, leads, orders, retention counts.

Occupancy can look healthy at every stage while **movement between stages** quietly fails — visibility without curiosity, curiosity without preference, intent without action, conversion without continuity.

Most teams diagnose funnel problems as traffic, content, or creative issues because they lack a governed language for **transition failure**.

The problem is not that funnels exist. The problem is that **transitions are unnamed, unmeasured, and undiagnosed.**

---

## 5. Why AIDA Needed a Transition Layer

AIDA remains structurally valid as a persuasion model: Attention, Interest, Desire, Action, Loyalty.

What classical funnel practice omitted was not AIDA itself — it was the **measurement grammar between states**.

AIDA did not fail.  
It was missing a transition layer.

That layer requires:

1. **Four governed transition vectors** (T1–T4) between five states,
2. **A fixed scoring standard** (ATI) for movement quality,
3. **A closed failure vocabulary** (TFO) for how movement weakens,
4. **Evidence Confidence** separate from score — qualifying interpretation, not replacing measurement.

AIDAtanaly exists to publish that layer as a reference system, not to replace AIDA with a new acronym game.
