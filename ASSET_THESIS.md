## 8. Layer 3 — Ontology: Transition Failure Ontology
 
The original classification system of the asset is the:
 
# **Transition Failure Ontology — TFO**
 
TFO is a governed taxonomy of movement failure modes organized under T1–T4.
 
Its purpose is to prevent vague funnel language.
 
Instead of saying:
 
 
“The funnel is not working.”
 
 
AIDAtanaly classifies the failure:
 
 
“This is a T2 Intent Formation failure caused by comparison stall and preference dilution.”
 
 
That is the difference between content and intelligence.
  
### 8.1 T1 Failure Modes — Attention → Interest
 
T1 failures occur when visibility does not become meaningful engagement.
 
Initial T1 classes:
 
 
- **Attention Noise** — exposure without qualified signal.
 
- **Vanity Reach** — large audience volume with weak transition probability.
 
- **Audience Mismatch** — attention from the wrong segment.
 
- **Signal Decay** — initial attention loses strength before interest forms.
 
- **Message Blur** — the audience sees the asset but cannot understand why it matters.
 

  
### 8.2 T2 Failure Modes — Interest → Desire
 
T2 failures occur when engagement does not crystallize into preference, intent, or motivation.
 
Initial T2 classes:
 
 
- **Passive Engagement** — users consume but do not move toward preference.
 
- **Comparison Stall** — prospects remain trapped in evaluation without preference formation.
 
- **Preference Dilution** — competing alternatives weaken brand selection.
 
- **Intent Evaporation** — early motivation disappears before commercial intent forms.
 
- **Value Ambiguity** — the user cannot translate interest into a reason to choose.
 

 
Preference and intent remain sub-signals inside T2, not separate transition vectors.
  
### 8.3 T3 Failure Modes — Desire → Action
 
T3 failures occur when formed desire fails to become action.
 
Initial T3 classes:
 
 
- **Process Friction** — the buying, signup, or contact process blocks movement.
 
- **Trust Deficit** — the prospect wants the offer but does not trust the next step.
 
- **Price Shock** — desire collapses when cost becomes explicit.
 
- **Decision Delay** — intent exists but urgency is weak.
 
- **Complexity Wall** — the user cannot complete action because the path is cognitively or operationally heavy.
 
- **Objection Residue** — unresolved doubts remain after desire forms.
 

  
### 8.4 T4 Failure Modes — Action → Loyalty
 
T4 failures occur when action does not become continuity.
 
Initial T4 classes:
 
 
- **One-Transaction Funnel** — conversion occurs but no repeat value forms.
 
- **Silent Churn** — users leave without clear visible complaint.
 
- **Loyalty Blindness** — the organization measures first action but not durability.
 
- **Advocacy Vacuum** — satisfied customers do not become referrers or advocates.
 
- **Lifecycle Disconnect** — post-action communication fails to continue the relationship.
 

 
Each failure mode must receive a permanent reference page with:
 
 
- definition,
 
- transition vector,
 
- symptoms,
 
- likely causes,
 
- detection signals,
 
- AI instrumentation,
 
- intervention layer,
 
- related failure modes,
 
- and canonical internal links.
 

 
This ontology is what makes AIDAtanaly a maker of market language rather than a follower of existing funnel vocabulary.
