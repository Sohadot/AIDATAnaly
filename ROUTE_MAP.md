## 1. Purpose of This Route Map

This document defines the governed public route architecture for AIDAtanaly.com.

Its purpose is to convert the ratified doctrine, standard, and ontology into a controlled public reference surface.

AIDAtanaly is not permitted to expand through random SEO pages, thin glossary pages, keyword harvesting, or disconnected marketing content.

Every route must serve at least one governed function:

1. define the category,
2. explain the framework,
3. support the ATI Standard,
4. expose the TFO Ontology,
5. power the scanner,
6. strengthen internal linking,
7. improve agent readability,
8. support monetization without degrading authority,
9. serve buyer logic,
10. or preserve governance and trust.

No route may exist without a structural reason.

---

## 2. Route Governance Principle

The route map follows the governing statement:

> Stages are states. Value lives in transitions.

Therefore, the public architecture must prioritize:

- transitions over stages,
- reference pages over blog posts,
- canonical definitions over generic commentary,
- diagnostic systems over vague content,
- governed internal linking over SEO randomness,
- and stable routes over temporary campaigns.

AIDAtanaly's route architecture must make the category legible to:

- human readers,
- search engines,
- AI agents,
- strategic buyers,
- and future scanner/API systems.

## 17. Route Creation Rules

A new route may be created only if at least one of the following is true:

1. It defines a governed term.
2. It explains an ATI vector.
3. It defines a TFO failure mode.
4. It supports scanner output.
5. It supports evidence confidence or intervention mapping.
6. It supports governance, methodology, or source trust.
7. It supports respectable monetization.
8. It supports buyer logic.
9. It provides machine-readable definitions.
10. It resolves an internal linking need.

A route may not be created only because:

- a keyword exists,
- a competitor has a page,
- an AI tool can generate it,
- a trend is popular,
- an affiliate opportunity exists,
- or a generic marketing topic is adjacent.

## 18. Broken Link Prevention

Before public release, every route must pass:

- all canonical links present,
- all parent links present,
- all child links present where applicable,
- all related failure mode links valid,
- all scanner-output links valid,
- sitemap contains only existing public routes,
- no internal link points to a deferred route unless clearly marked as coming soon or omitted,
- no public route links to repository-only files as public pages unless intentionally mirrored.

No broken links are permitted.

---

## 19. Deferred Route Handling

Deferred routes must not appear as live public links unless:

- the route exists as a placeholder with substantial explanation,
- it is intentionally marked as future,
- it does not produce thin content,
- and it is included in sitemap only after it becomes meaningful.

Default rule:

> Deferred means not linked publicly until built.

## 20. Route Governance and Change Control

Route changes are classified as follows.

### 20.1 Patch Change

Small correction that does not affect route meaning.

Examples:

- typo in route description,
- priority adjustment,
- minor internal link update.

### 20.2 Minor Change

Additive route change that preserves architecture.

Examples:

- adding an early expansion route,
- adding a report route,
- adding a data endpoint,
- adding a new intervention page.

### 20.3 Major Change

Structural change that affects route logic.

Examples:

- changing canonical route structure,
- renaming `/failure-modes/`,
- moving vector routes,
- removing required launch pages,
- changing indexation policy,
- changing route class definitions.

Major route changes require:

- decision log entry,
- route impact statement,
- sitemap impact statement,
- internal link impact statement,
- scanner impact statement where applicable.
