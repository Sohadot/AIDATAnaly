Scanner v1.0 - rules-governed

AIDAtanaly Transition Scanner

Scanner v1 is rules-governed. It measures transition health, qualifies evidence, classifies likely failure modes, and links diagnosis to governed reference pages.

What the Scanner Measures

The scanner evaluates movement quality across the four ATI transition vectors (T1-T4) using the seven weighted dimensions defined in the ATI Standard. It assigns Evidence Confidence per vector, names likely TFO failure modes, and recommends governed intervention layers.

Scoring rules, question models, thresholds, and failure mode mappings are loaded from /data/scanner-model.json and the governed data registry. No score logic is hard-coded outside those files.

What the Scanner Does Not Claim

Scanner v1 is rules-governed. It does not require live analytics integrations and does not claim exact causality.

  • It does not prove why revenue is falling.
  • It does not promise conversion growth or revenue outcomes.
  • It does not replace measurement systems or CRM data.
  • It is not described as an adopted industry standard.

Evidence Confidence Notice

Evidence Confidence qualifies how strongly a result should be interpreted.

Evidence Confidence is not part of the ATI score. E0 (Absent Evidence) means a vector is Unscorable - no numeric vector score is issued. When one or more vectors are E0, Scanner v1 must issue a Partial Profile rather than a clean composite ATI score.

See Evidence Confidence and Measurement Gap for the governed constraint rules.
