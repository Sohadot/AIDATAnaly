Methodology

This page explains how the governed reference system works in plain language: what is measured, what is classified, and what is explicitly not claimed.

Governing Thesis

Stages are states. Value lives in transitions.

AIDA Transition Analytics treats funnel movement as the unit of intelligence. AIDAtanaly does not optimize isolated stage counts; it measures whether prospects move from Attention to Interest, Interest to Desire, Desire to Action, and Action to Loyalty.

AIDAtanaly measures transition health. It does not claim guaranteed causality or guaranteed revenue improvement.

Why Transitions Matter

Stage metrics describe what happened inside a state: impressions, sessions, leads, orders. Transition metrics describe whether movement between states is healthy, fast, resilient, and commercially meaningful. When a transition weakens, the break has a location (T1-T4), a failure class (TFO), and an intervention layer - not only a vague underperformance label.

How ATI Scores Movement

The AIDA Transition Index (ATI) scores four transition vectors (T1-T4). Each vector score is a weighted composite of seven dimensions (D1-D7): Signal Quality, Movement Probability, Transition Velocity, Friction Resilience, Drop-Off Resilience, Commercial Consequence, and Intervention Clarity.

When all four vectors are scorable, a composite ATI score may be reported as the average of T1-T4. Dimension weights and diagnostic class thresholds are defined in the ratified standard and mirrored in /data/ati-standard.json.

How Evidence Confidence Works

Evidence Confidence (E0-E4) qualifies how strongly any score should be interpreted. It is mandatory, reported separately, and never part of the ATI score mathematically.

  • E0 - Absent Evidence: vector is Unscorable; no numeric score.
  • E1-E4 - progressively stronger evidence, never implying guaranteed outcomes.

If one or more vectors are E0, output becomes a Partial Profile rather than a clean composite score.

How TFO Classifies Failure

The Transition Failure Ontology (TFO) names governed failure patterns when movement is weak: 21 diagnostic dossiers across T1-T4, plus Measurement Gap as a cross-vector diagnostic constraint. ATI answers how strong movement is; TFO answers what class of movement failure is present.

How Scanner v1 Produces Output

The Transition Scanner is a rules-governed diagnostic engine. It loads questions, weights, thresholds, and failure mode mappings from /data/scanner-model.json and related registry files. It does not require live analytics integrations and does not claim exact causality.

Output includes per-vector scores (when scorable), Evidence Confidence, likely failure modes with links to dossier pages, recommended intervention layers, and reference links to governed documentation.

What the System Does Not Claim

  • No guaranteed revenue, conversion, or growth outcomes.
  • No exact causal proof from a single scan.
  • No adopted industry-standard status without adoption evidence (see Sources).
  • No replacement for measurement systems, CRM, or professional advisory services.

Versioning and Governance

Standards, ontology, scanner model, and interface rules are versioned and ratified under Governance. Change severity is recorded in decision logs; the public site reflects ratified versions only.
