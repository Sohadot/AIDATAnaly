Evidence Confidence

Every output of the AIDA Transition Index carries a second, separate reading: how much evidence stands behind it. That reading is Evidence Confidence, expressed on a five-level scale from E0 to E4.

Governing Rule

Evidence Confidence is not part of the ATI score.

Evidence Confidence is reported separately from the score, displayed separately from the score, and never included in the score mathematically. There is no evidence weight inside the scoring formula. A score answers how healthy is the movement? Evidence Confidence answers a different question: how strongly should that answer be believed?

Why This Standard Exists

Diagnostic systems lose integrity in one of two ways: they refuse to say anything until data is perfect, or they present guesses with the same certainty as measurements. Evidence Confidence is the mechanism that avoids both. It lets ATI score what can honestly be scored, while making the strength of the underlying evidence permanently visible next to every number — never blended into it.

The Five Levels

LevelNameMeaningOutput treatment
E0 Absent Evidence No meaningful evidence available. Unscorable. No numeric score is issued.
E1 Self-Reported Evidence Based mainly on user answers or internal belief. Score allowed, described in provisional language.
E2 Observable Evidence Based on visible metrics, behavior, or reported funnel data. Score allowed, described in indicative language.
E3 Multi-Source Evidence Based on multiple signals across channels or systems. Stronger confidence language allowed; no guaranteed causality.
E4 Validated Evidence Based on tracked, repeated, and verifiable data patterns. Strongest confidence language allowed; no guaranteed causality.

Assignment logic, in one line: no evidence is E0, belief alone is E1, one observable source is E2, multiple connected sources are E3, and validated repeated data is E4. Confidence is assigned per vector, not once per scan.

The E0 Rule: Unscorable Means Unscored

When a vector has no meaningful evidence, ATI does not estimate, does not average, and does not display a zero. Zero is a score; absence of evidence is not. The vector renders as an explicit Unscorable state:

T4: Unscorable E0 Absent Evidence

No numeric score is issued for this vector. Measurement Gap limits the confidence of this diagnosis.

Prohibited rendering: T4: 22 / E0 Absent Evidence. A number next to E0 is a false measurement.

The Partial Profile Rule

The E0 rule extends to the composite score. If one or more vectors are E0, the scanner must not issue a clean composite ATI score. It issues a Partial Profile instead:

ATI Profile: Partial
Scorable vectors: T1, T2, T3
Unscorable vector: T4 / E0 Absent Evidence

Averaging three measured vectors with one unmeasured vector would manufacture a composite number that no evidence supports. The Partial Profile keeps the output honest while still delivering full diagnostic value for the vectors that can be scored.

Language Discipline by Level

  • E0 — no numeric score; the vector is named Unscorable and the measurement gap is stated.
  • E1 — results use provisional language: "this diagnosis is based on self-reported evidence and should be treated as provisional."
  • E2 — results use indicative language: "observable evidence indicates weak movement on this vector."
  • E3 and E4 — results may use stronger confidence language; language implying guaranteed causality or promised outcomes remains prohibited at every level, including E4.

How It Appears in the Interface

The separation is visual, not only textual. Scores use the diagnostic class scale; Evidence Confidence always renders as a neutral badge adjacent to — never inside — the score visual:

58 Transition Unstable E2 Observable Evidence

The colored panel is the score. The gray badge is the confidence. They never merge.

Relationship to Measurement Gap

Low Evidence Confidence is frequently the surface symptom of Measurement Gap (tfo.constraint.measurement_gap) — the diagnostic constraint in the Transition Failure Ontology that applies when an organization cannot measure its own movement clearly. Measurement Gap does not reduce any ATI score. It reduces the confidence with which scores may be interpreted, which is exactly what this scale records.

What This Standard Does Not Claim

  • High confidence does not mean a high score, and a high score does not mean high confidence. The two readings are independent by design.
  • E4 does not certify business success; it certifies that the evidence behind the diagnosis is tracked, repeated, and verifiable.
  • No confidence level converts a diagnosis into a promise. Unsupported certainty and revenue promises are prohibited at every level.
