ATI Snapshot

A structured export of governed transition diagnosis - an extension of the free scanner, not a separate black-box score.

What the ATI Snapshot Provides

The ATI Snapshot packages the same governed logic as Scanner v1 into a readable, shareable document: composite or Partial Profile status, per-vector results, Evidence Confidence summary, likely TFO failure modes, and recommended intervention layers with links to reference pages.

Who It Is For

  • Operators who completed a scanner pass and need an audit-friendly summary.
  • Teams aligning marketing, product, and lifecycle around the same transition language.
  • Advisors who need governed definitions alongside diagnostic output.

It is not positioned as a replacement for analytics platforms or professional consulting.

What It Includes

  • Composite status or Partial Profile (when E0 applies).
  • T1-T4 vector results with diagnostic class where scorable.
  • Evidence Confidence per vector and summary.
  • Weakest and strongest vector callouts.
  • Likely failure modes with stable IDs and dossier links.
  • Recommended intervention layers (class level).
  • Reference links to ATI, TFO, Evidence Confidence, and methodology.

What It Does Not Claim

  • No guaranteed revenue, conversion, or growth outcomes.
  • No exact causal proof that a failure mode caused a revenue change.
  • No industry-standard or market-adoption status without evidence.
  • No urgency framing or fear-based pressure to purchase upgrades.

Evidence Confidence Treatment

Every section separates score from confidence. E0 vectors appear as Unscorable with no numeric score. E1 uses provisional language; E2 indicative; E3/E4 stronger but never guaranteed causality. See Evidence Confidence.

Relationship to Scanner

The snapshot is generated from the same rules-governed inputs and registries as the on-page scanner. It does not introduce new failure modes, weights, or thresholds. Users should complete the Transition Scanner first; the report organizes that output for retention and sharing.

Relationship to ATI and TFO

Scores follow the ATI Standard. Failure names follow the Transition Failure Ontology only. Each named failure mode links to its canonical dossier route.

Example Output Sections

Composite ATI: 68 — Transition Unstable
Composite Evidence Confidence: E2 Observable Evidence

T2 — Interest → Desire / Score: 52 / E1 Self-Reported Evidence
Likely Failure Modes: Value Ambiguity (tfo.t2.value_ambiguity)

ATI Profile: Partial (example when T4 is E0)
Scorable vectors: T1, T2, T3

Illustrative structure only. Actual output follows governed scanner rules.

Trust and Limits

Access to a formatted snapshot may be offered as a paid or registered export in a future release. Basic definitions remain on public reference pages without payment. No forced lead capture is required to understand what the report contains.

Paid diagnostic briefs and audits, when available, must use the same claim discipline. See Governance, Methodology, and Terms.
